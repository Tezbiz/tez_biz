What is One Person Company (OPC) ?

For the first time in India, the concept of One Person Company was introduced by the Companies Act, 2013 which allows a single person to incorporate a Company and start business. The OPC is going to be popular option for the India amongst the small business houses. The main advantages of the One Person Company (OPC) is that there can be only one member in a OPC whereas minimum of two members are mandatory for incorporating and maintaining a Private Limited Company or a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP). Moreover, the OPC enjoys the benefit available to the Company also like a separate legal entity from its promoter, offering limited liability protection to its sole shareholder, while having continuity of business and being easy to incorporate etc.

It is important for the Entrepreneur to carefully consider the features of a One Person Company prior to incorporation, viz. 1. OPC allows Entrepreneur to operate a corporate entity with limited liability protection, 2. every One Person Company (OPC) must nominate a nominee Director in the MOA and AOA of the company - who will become the promoter of the OPC in case the sole Director is disabled. 3. a One Person Company must be converted into a Private Limited Company if it crosses an annual turnover of Rs.2 crores or cross the paid up capital of Rsw. 50 lacs, 4. Required to file audited financial statements with the Registrar of Companies every year.

Main Advantages of One Person Company (OPC)

  • Most appropriate for businesses with single promoter willing to step into Corporate World
  • Liability of the owner is limited to the extent of capital
  • Appointment of Nominee for enjoying Undisturbed Existence of the company and perpetual succession
  • Centralized registration and Transparency in transactions
  • Higher flexibility and less compliance requirements compared to Private Company
  • Hire Managerial Personnel without affecting share in ownership

  • As like other Company, separation of management and ownership is also possible in OPC
  • Ease in Raising capital from Financial Institutions
  • Ease of Operations and administration
  • Higher Creditability from other organizations alike Private Company
  • Banks and Financial Institutions prefer to provide funding to a OPC rather than partnership firms or proprietary concerns.
  • OPC can acquire, own, enjoy and alienate, property in its name.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the basic requisites for One Person Company (OPC) Registration in India?

To register One Person Company (OPC) in India, the DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) and DIN (Director Identification Number) of all the directors and Subscriber to MOA (owner) along with the Nominee is to be acquired. The Registered Office shall also be required for online Private Limited Company Registration.

What is a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) ? Who shall procure DSC for One Person Company Registration online ?

Digital Signature Certificate is provided in a token form and issued by Certified Authorities. Any form filed to register an OPC in India shall be submitted after affixing the DSC of an Applicant. Also, the directors will require DSC for DIN application and the subscribers to MOA along with the Nominee shall possess DSC for submitting e-forms.

What is DIN (Director Identification Number) ?

Director Identification Number is a unique number assigned by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to Individuals on application made, which allows any individual to be Director in any Company or Designated Partner in LLP and Nominee at the One Person Company in India.

What amount of Capital is required for OPC Registration in India?

OPC can be incorporated with any amount of Paid-up Capital, in India. Nevertheless, the paid-up capital shall not exceed Rupees 50 Lakh as it attracts mandatory conversion into Private or Public Company.

What are Authorized Capital and Paid-up Capital?

Authorized capital shows maximum amount of capital, a company can raise by way of issue of shares at present or in future whereas the Paid-up Capital refers to the actual amount raised by and introduced in the Limited Company i.e. paid by shareholders to the Limited Company on issuance of shares. A company can be incorporated by any amount of Paid-up capital, which can be less that authorized capital but not exceeding such.

What is the number of natural person required for registration of One Person Company (OPC) in India?

As the name suggest, there is only one promoter i.e. One Shareholder in the One Person Company. Further, one nominee is to be appointed with his consent. With regards to Director, appointment of minimum 1 Director is also prescribed. Here, the Shareholder and the Director can be the same person. However, here more than one Director can also be appointed.

Who is the nominee in One Person Company?

The requirement of appointment of nominee is provided by the Government in order to retrain the character of Perpetual Existence i.e. Uninterrupted Existence of the One Person Company. A nominee shall be individual only and is to be appointed while incorporation of the OPC. In event of death or incapacity to contract of existing member, the nominee will become member of the one person company.

What are the requirements to be a member or nominee for One Person Company Registration in India ?

The member/nominee shall be an individual being Resident of India. Further, he or she shall not be minor or incompetent to contract. In addition to same, the Nominee shall hold DIN (as explained above) and give his consent to company for appointment as nominee.

What are the requirements to be a Director in the company?

There are no limitations in terms of citizenship or residency to be a director. But the person should be 18 or above 18 years of age i.e. not a minor. Also, the proposed director shall have DIN (as explained above) and not be disqualified to be appointed as director in Limited Company as per the provisions of Indian Companies Act, 2013.

Can a Body Corporate register One Person Company in India ?

No. Only an individual can be a member in the One Person Company.

Whether a Body Corporate can be appointed as a Nominee for OPC Registration?

A nominee in the OPC shall be an individual only and not a Body Corporate. Further, the consent of the Nominee is also required in the format provided for appointment.

What are the documents required for the One Person Company Registration in India?

Identity proof and address proof of the subscriber to MOA, Nominee and the proposed director is mandatory requirement for registration of OPC in India. In addition, the landlord of the registered office premises must provide a No Objection Certificate for having the registered office in his/her premises and must submit his/her identity proof and address proof.

Whether a place of Business is mandatory for registration of OPC?

Registered Office shall be the mandatory requirement in India and it shall be counted as the place of business in India.. The premises can be a commercial / industrial / residential where communication from the various governmental authorities will be received.

Can I convert OPC into Private Limited Company after registration of One Person Company (OPC)?

An OPC can be converted into Private or Public Company only after passing of 2 years from the date of Incorporation unless otherwise crossing of the threshold limit.

When is it compulsory to convert One Person Company in Private or Public Limited Company?

When the paid-up capital of the OPC exceeds 50 lakh Rupees, or Average Annual Turnover during relevant period exceeds 2 Crore Rupees, the OPC must be converted into Pvt Ltd Company or Public Ltd Company. The mandatory conversion will take effect irrespective of period of existence of OPC.

Can I register two or more One Person Company in India?

No, the individual can be the owner of only One OPC. Hence, he/she cannot register two or more One Person Company at the same time.

Whether I should be present in person to incorporate a One Person Company?

No, you will not have to be present at our office or appear at any office for the incorporation of a One Person Company. All the documents can be uploaded online due to centralized online registration portal provided by Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

How long does it take for One Person Company registration online?

TEJ can incorporate a One Person Company in one working day also provide that the valid documents are submitted, the name is unique and government processing time. The time taken for incorporation will depend on submission of relevant documents by the client and response of Government Approvals. To ensure speedy incorporation, please choose a unique name for your Company and ensure you have all the required documents prior to starting the incorporation process.

How long the incorporation and registration of the One Person Company is valid for?

Once a company whether being an OPC or otherwise, has a unique characteristic of uninterrupted existence. Once a Company is incorporated, it will be active and in-existence as long as the annual compliances are met with regularly. In case, annual compliances are not complied with, the Company will become a Dormant Company and maybe struck off from the register after a period of time. A struck-off Company can be revived for a period of up to 20 years.

What should be taken care for quick incorporation of One Person Company online?

The promoter of the company should make sure that the proposed name of the OPC for online registration is very unique. Further, all the documents with respect to the Subscriber, Nominee and Directors as well as Registered Office shall be as per the requirement.

What are the statutory compliances requirements to be fulfilled after one person company registration in India?

Alike a Private Limited Company, an OPC shall hold a Board Meeting and an AGM. The Meeting of Board of Directors shall be held at least once in each half of the calendar year, where the gap between two meetings shall not be more than 90 days. Fulfilment of Annual Compliance Requirements is must to maintain active status of the One Person Company in India.

Whether GST Registration is required for the Private Limited Company?

Alike every other entity, there are certain criteria laid down for mandatory registration under GST. Following are few general criteria for mandatory registration:

  • An aggregate turnover exceeding Rs. 20 lakh in a financial year mandates registration for a dealer or service provider under this law. However, for dealers conducting business in north-eastern states of India, an aggregate turnover exceeding Rs. 10 lakh mandates a registration.
  • Any online platform operator.

Whether a Trademark Application is mandatory for the name of Company?

No, the trademark Application is not mandatory. The name of the One Person Company itself is unique and the same name is not allotted to any other body-corporate being any company or LLP as their name. Beside this protection, you can voluntarily apply for the Trademark registration to protect name form unauthorised usage by Sole Proprietor and Partnership Firms, etc.
